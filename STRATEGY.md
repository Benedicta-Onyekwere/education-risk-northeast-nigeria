# Strategy Section: Mapping Education Risk in Northeastern Nigeria

### Context
Northeastern Nigeria has experienced over fifteen years of sustained armed conflict. Millions of children in this region have had their education interrupted repeatedly as conflict dynamics shift. While EBI field teams possess deep local knowledge, the organization lacks a tool to visualize this risk at scale across the regional conflict corridor.

### Objectives
1.  **Reveal the geography of education risk:** Overlaying conflict intensity and child population.
2.  **Give EBI staff a usable planning tool:** An interactive dashboard for prioritization.
3.  **Document a replicable methodology:** For use in EBI’s other 11 country programs.

### Approach
The core argument of this proposal is that mapping verified schools against conflict lethality and child population identifies the most critical intervention points.
*   **Conflict:** 265 events (UCDP 2024).
*   **Schools:** 76 ground-truthed facilities (HOT-OSM).
*   **Population:** 2020 UNFPA projections (5-14 age band).

### Use of Technology & AI
*   **AI for Data Engineering:** Used LLMs to generate Python scripts for spatial joins and data validation.
*   **Systematic Quality:** Employed OpenRefine for advanced cleaning of the school dataset.
*   **v0.app & Leaflet.js:** Rapidly prototyped a high-fidelity dashboard for non-technical users.

### Deliverables
1.  **Interactive Dashboard:** Live web-map for regional planning.
2.  **Cleaned Datasets:** Verified CSV files with a Data Dictionary.
3.  **Methodology Note:** Full documentation of the 35/45/20 risk weighting.
